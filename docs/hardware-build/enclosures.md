# Enclosures

> Optional cases and protectors for your SeedSigner device — 3D-printed, acrylic, or none at all.

An enclosure protects your SeedSigner device and gives it a more polished feel, but it is entirely optional.

## 3D-printed enclosures

The SeedSigner project maintains several community-designed, 3D-printable enclosure options in the GitHub repository:

**[SeedSigner Enclosures on GitHub](https://github.com/SeedSigner/seedsigner/tree/dev/enclosures)**

Each design in that directory includes its own documentation describing:

- Which specific components it accommodates (standard vs. compact camera, Pi Zero vs. other models)
- Print settings and material recommendations
- Assembly notes specific to that enclosure

If you have access to a 3D printer — or know someone who does, or can use a local library or makerspace — this is the most popular route.

> **Tip:** Browse the enclosure designs **before** you order your components. Your enclosure choice may determine which camera form factor (standard or compact) you should buy. It is easier to match the camera to the case than the other way around.

## Barebones alternative

If you do not have access to a 3D printer, there is a simpler option: commercially available **acrylic protector plates** designed for the Raspberry Pi Zero. These are two clear acrylic sheets that sandwich the Pi, held together with small screws and standoff nuts.

For this setup, use the **compact form-factor Pi Zero camera** so that the camera board sits neatly within the footprint of the Pi. The acrylic plates protect the board from shorts and physical damage while keeping the build slim and simple.

These acrylic kits are inexpensive and available from most Pi retailers and general electronics shops.

## No enclosure at all

There is nothing wrong with running SeedSigner without any enclosure. Many people start this way while they are learning the device, and some never bother with a case at all. Just be mindful of where you set the bare board down — avoid metal surfaces or anything conductive that could short the exposed pins on the underside.

> **Warning:** If you operate without an enclosure, handle the device by its edges and avoid touching the electronic components directly. Static discharge or accidental shorts can damage the board.
